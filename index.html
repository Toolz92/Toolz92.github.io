Projects

Exorcism At Dusckridge Manor

This is the game I helped develop at Nocturnal Knights. It is a two-player, third-person, asymmetrical game of cat-and-mouse. One player acts as a detective trying to solve the mysteries of the manor, and the other acts as the ghost attempting to drive the detective insane. The detective must search the house looking for evidence, and once 5 pieces have been found, head towards the final altar to banish the ghost. The ghost must drive off the detective by getting close enough to him to drain his sanity, and the ghost must drain all the sanity before the detective can accomplish his goal. Both players have a variety of resources and tools to hinder the other, such as locking doors or placing repelling wards.

Flappy Bullet

This is a project I worked on over the summer during breaks in my regular job. I wanted to challenge myself to only use what I knew (so, no Googling) and assets already on my computer, hence the heavy use of Flappy Bird assets. Flappy Bullet is a top-down shoot'em'up game with enemies coming towards you from the top of the screen. This project was fun to make as it pushed my creativity and problem-solving with a time limit, as well as gave me a chance to try out asset creation. All the assets that aren't from Flappy Bird are created by me in a pixel art creator.

Sci-Fi Maximus

Built in Unreal Engine 3, the goal of this project was to create a 2D brawler in the same vein as Streets of Rage. With that gameplay concept in mind, we created a short, two-player demo packed with 3 playable charecters, 3 enemy types, and 3 levels to play through. This project was graded based on if it was accepted to be published through the ID@Xbox system; therefore, it required much stricter coding and mandatory Xbox functionality support, such as gamertags. At the end of the course, it was submitted for publication and was accepted. It gained over 200 downloads in its first week and many positve reviews.
